How to Get More Reviews For Your Tree Company

Get more reviews for your tree company by implementing a system to consistently ask customers for a review after the job was completed, respond back to all reviews within 24 hours, and have a page available on your website so that customers trying to leave you a review can easily find what sites your business is listed on.

Want This Done For You?

Ask Customers In Person

When is the most likely point that you will get a customer review? Right after the job was completed. If you wait a day or two before sending the customer a text asking for a review, you will get fewer reviews compared to if you just asked in person. When we start working with a new business on developing a customer review strategy, we give them a QR code linked directly to their Google Business Profile. Once the customer says they will give a review, have them scan the QR code to leave a review. It makes it completely frictionless and you will get many more reviews.

Send a Follow Up via Email/SMS

Was the customer not available after you completed the job? Normally when this happens, we suggest leaving a flyer that has the QR code to leave you a review and marking them as needing text follow up in your CRM. You can either manually send a text to the client after completing the job or use software to automate this process.

Respond Back to All Reviews

Just getting reviews isn't enough, you should respond back to all of them. Responding back to positive reviews helps show customers that you are reading the reviews and it gives you a chance to showcase your customer service. If you get a bad review, responding allows you to show your professionalism and publicly offer to make it right. If future customers are confident you will address any concerns they have, they will trust you more with their business.

Put Review Links On Your Website

The last step to getting consistent, good reviews is to leave a link to all of your review sites somewhere on your website. If a past customer wanted to leave a review and they go to your website to do so, you want to make it easy for them to find where they can do that.

Frequently Asked Questions

1

Should I only get reviews on Google? Or other review sites too?

Having reviews on multiple platforms helps prove that your reviews are real and it helps customers trust you more. In the United States, Apple Maps uses Yelp reviews so if someone looks you up there and you have no reviews, that won't help you. Typically, we start by building up reviews on Google and then begin moving our focus to other platforms once reviews are no longer the limiting factor on our Google rankings.

2

Can you handle this whole process for me?

Yes, in our complete marketing package, we handle setting up the QR code/flyer, responding back to reviews, and setting up an automated text/email outreach for any customers who didn't leave a review at the job site.

3

How many reviews do I need on Google?

There is no set number, review frequency is also very important. You want consistent fresh reviews on your profile. Managing your online reputation doesn't have an end, it is an ongoing task that you need to focus on for as long as you are in business.
